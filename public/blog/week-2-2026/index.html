Week 02/2026

2026-01-11 · Mihir Pathak

5th Jan to 11th Jan 2026 (Mon to Sunday)

Monday: Office work

Tuesday: Office work

Wednesday: Had a short meeting with Samagata Foundation Team about the children’s library project.

Thursday: Vaibhav bhai and Soma didi visited Ahmedabad. Soma didi is working on a project based on ‘semantic grammar’. We had a session at the Community Education Center with the help of Mahendra sir.

Friday: Office work. In the evening, I had a call with an artist called Ishani for my documentation project.

Saturday:

  • Had a short catch-up call with Srishti.
  • Had a call with Sajhe Sapne alumni—it was an orientation call to see if they want to contribute to the documentation project I’m doing for my past project.
  • In the evening, I had a call with Nandini from Nazaria Art Collective. We discussed each other’s work and the possibility of me contributing to their summer film school curriculum, bringing ideas from self-directed learning, project-based learning, and related stuff.
  • In the evening, I met my dear friend Harshal who publishes Kulfi children’s magazine in Marathi, and one more friend, Raj, who works with Parag - Tata Trust.

Sunday: Vaibhav bhai was here at home last night. It was his birthday on Sunday, so we went to Arvind Sadhna Kendra near our home. After that, me, Vaibhav bhai, and Mummy went to have lunch at Kamla Cafe. In the evening, we went to Navjivan Trust Campus to attend Navjivan Talks. This time it was about the memory of an interesting publisher called ‘Shivji Ashar’. It is really happy to see that we are celebrating very interesting Publisher of Gujarati language. Me and Raam also had a chat about starting a children’s reading club during their Sunday reading club happening regularly at the Navjivan campus. He was excited about the idea. Me and Nidhi will start it on a Sunday after Makar Sankranti. Vaibhav bhai left for Mumbai from there.

  • For the next few months, I am going to focus on the documentation project. My dear friend Rahul Hasija helped me with this. Now I am finding a team to execute the whole thing.
  • We will start a children’s reading club at Navjivan Trust Campus (I have applied to Samagata Foundation and Sudha Mahesh Grants for this project—let’s see how it works out).
  • What else? Will see how Nidhi will start her work after she comes back from Kanpur.
  • Apart from that, things at home are silent right now. Don’t know what will happen next.
  • Nidhi will come on Tuesday. I will take a break from work during 14th and 15th Jan and spend good time with her.

I am overall very excited about how my and Nidhi’s work is getting clarity and space in this big chaotic world.

We are facilitating experiential learning journeys for children and young adults:

1. Nature Detective (3 to 11 yrs)

  • I am engaging this age group in my full-time job at Balgokulam.
  • Nidhi will engage this age group if she joins CEE full-time.
  • We are going to start a reading club soon. (similar age group)
  • Working with Children’s University team to develop integrated learning modules.
  • Apart from this, I want to write stories, poems, and drama for children and work on Gujarati children’s literature actively.

2. Local Khoji (12+)

  • I am actively looking for orgs where I can contribute/engage with this age group. I have already found two orgs, so I will actively share my work with them.
  • Will be focusing on making a playbooks/documentation/publishing.

I am worried about the future—what types of jobs will be there, how we will survive, how to earn enough money so we can support our old parents. Lots of questions and insecurities are there, but somewhat there’s trust in the universe. Let’s see.

We were also thinking of moving to Bhopal so Nidhi can visit her home more often to see her family. But it is difficult to get work in Bhopal. Also, Ahmedabad is a great place to work if I want to collaborate with Gujarati artists and other educators from here. Let’s see. Will figure out something. For the next one year, I think I want to be here so I can document my work and be ready for the transition.

My mental health is overall low, but I will work on it in the coming months.
